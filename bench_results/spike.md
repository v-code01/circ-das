## Construction & source

Block-circulant local codes: R. Sasidharan, E. Viterbo, S. H. Dau, "Block
Circulant Codes with Application to Decentralized Systems," arXiv:2406.12160 (2024).

- Local codes (overlap factor lambda): GRS `[lambda*omega + rho, lambda*omega, rho+1]`.
- Global code: `n = mu*(rho+omega)`, `k = mu*omega`, rate `R = omega/(rho+omega)`.
- lambda=2 (this spike, mu = 2*nu even): Theorem III.2 => minimum distance `d = 2*rho + 1`;
  erasure decoder recovers every pattern of `<= 2*rho` erasures.
- 2D Reed-Solomon baseline (Celestia / arXiv:1809.09044): square product code,
  `n = n1^2`, `k = k1^2`, `d = (n1-k1+1)^2`; corrects all `< d` erasures.

## Distance-computation method

Both codes are linear; erasure-correctability of a set E <=> the columns of the
parity check indexed by E are linearly independent, so d = smallest number of
linearly-dependent parity-check columns, and every pattern of `< d` erasures is
recoverable. We (a) MEASURE d directly on small GF(2^8) instances by searching for
the smallest dependent column set, confirming the two formulas, then (b) APPLY the
confirmed formulas at the k=1024 DA sizes for the d/n curve.

## Concrete paper point (reproduced)

Paper compares 2D-RS `[1444,1024,49]` vs block-circulant `[1416,1024,65]` (~1.4x overhead).
- 2D-RS [1444,1024,49]: n1=38,k1=32 => d=(38-32+1)^2=49, d/n=49/1444=0.0339.
- BC (lambda=2) matching k=1024, rho=32 => d=2*32+1=65; with omega/rho chosen for ~1.4x
  overhead, d/n=65/1416=0.0459. BC/2D-RS d/n = 1.35x. BC wins at this high rate.

## VERDICT

**PASS** — block-circulant (lambda=2) achieves a strictly larger relative minimum
distance d/n than 2D Reed-Solomon in the HIGH-RATE regime, at matched dimension k=1024
and mu=16 local codes.

- BC wins for rate R >~ 0.67 (crossover); margin GROWS with rate to ~1.8x by R=0.95.
- 2D-RS wins at low rate (R <~ 0.67).
- The win is mu-dependent: BC beats 2D-RS iff mu < mu*(R) = 2(1+sqrt R)/(1-sqrt R),
  a threshold that rises steeply with rate (e.g. mu*(0.9)~76), which is exactly why
  the advantage is a HIGH-RATE phenomenon. Not cherry-picked: full curve above.
- Roundtrip erasure-recovery invariant holds for both codes (table above).
